# Three Hunters + Support Fellowship

← [[games/lotr-lcg/index|Back to LOTR LCG]]

> The recommended two-handed solo fellowship for your collection. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli handle all combat and generate quest progress through kills. Eowyn, Theodred, and Ori cover everything else — questing hard, feeding Aragorn resources every round, and keeping both hands full of cards.

## Why this fellowship works

**Theodred solves Deck A's core problem.** The Three Hunters have two Tactics heroes — lots of red resources, never enough Leadership blue. Aragorn generates just 1 Leadership resource per round normally. Theodred passes 1 resource to another hero every round he quests — always send it to Aragorn. This effectively doubles Deck A's Leadership economy every single turn for free, funding Steward of Gondor, Sneak Attack, and expensive allies early.

**Eowyn covers all the questing Deck A can't.** Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli are fighters. Eowyn has the highest base willpower in your pool at 4. Her discard ability lets her spike willpower when you need to clear a location or push through a tough quest stage fast.

**Legolas turns combat into questing.** Every enemy Legolas kills places 2 progress tokens on the quest. With Deck B questing hard and Legolas farming kills, you advance the quest from two directions simultaneously — this is what makes the fellowship fast and efficient.

**Ori provides card draw across both decks.** Drawing a card every time you play a Dwarf ally keeps both hands full. In two-handed solo this matters enormously since you're managing 100 cards between two decks.

**A Test of Will in Deck B is your safety net.** Always hold one in hand. The most dangerous moment in any scenario is a surprise Treachery revealing at the wrong time — this cancels it entirely.

## Deck A — The Three Hunters

### Heroes

**Aragorn** (Leadership) — Threat 12
Quests, then readies himself to defend or attack the same round. The most flexible hero in your pool. With Steward of Gondor attached he generates 3 resources per round — the fuel for everything in Deck A. Priority target for every buff.

**Legolas** (Tactics) — Threat 9
Every enemy he kills places 2 progress tokens on the active quest location or quest card. Combat is questing. Equip him with a weapon early and every fight advances your objective without spending any additional actions.

**Gimli** (Tactics) — Threat 11
Gets +1 Attack for each damage token on him. Let him take hits intentionally — a Gimli with 4 damage on him and a Dwarven Axe attacks for 6+ and one-shots most enemies in the core set. Pair with Feint to control when he takes damage.

### Key cards for Deck A

| Card | Sphere | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Steward of Gondor | Leadership | On Aragorn — 3 resources per round, priority one |
| Unexpected Courage | Spirit | On Aragorn — quest AND defend/attack same round |
| Dwarven Axe | Tactics | On Gimli — boosts his already high attack |
| Feint | Tactics | Cancel an enemy attack entirely for the round |
| Quick Strike | Tactics | Attack before an enemy strikes — kill it safely |
| Sneak Attack + Gandalf | Leadership | 1 resource for Gandalf's -5 threat option — use when climbing toward 45+ |
| Rivendell Blade | Tactics | On Legolas — reduces enemy defense, easier kills, more progress tokens |
| Foe-hammer | Tactics | Draw 3 cards when you kill an enemy with a weapon — insane draw engine with Legolas |

### How to play Deck A

**Early (rounds 1-3):**
Get Steward of Gondor on Aragorn — this is the non-negotiable priority. Commit Aragorn to questing then ready him with his ability for defense. Let Gimli take a hit or two to start building his attack bonus. Use Feint to protect Aragorn and Legolas until Gimli is set up as primary defender.

**Mid (rounds 4-7):**
Equip Legolas with a weapon. Every kill now drops 2 progress tokens — you start winning the quest through combat. Sneak Attack Gandalf when combined threat approaches 45. Gimli with 3+ damage is now your premier attacker — point him at the biggest enemy every round.

**Late:**
Aragorn with Unexpected Courage quests, readies, defends, then attacks — he does everything in a single round. Gimli with 4-5 damage and Dwarven Axe attacks for 7+ and one-shots almost anything. Legolas rains progress tokens with every kill.

## Deck B — The Support

### Heroes

**Eowyn** (Spirit) — Threat 9
Primary quester. Base 4 willpower — the highest in your pool. Discard any card from hand to boost willpower by 1 each — use this to clear a location fast or push through a tough quest stage on a key round. Never commit her to combat.

**Theodred** (Leadership) — Threat 8
Every round he commits to questing, pass his generated resource to another hero. Always pass to Aragorn in Deck A — no exceptions. His entire purpose is funding the expensive Leadership cards in Deck A. He contributes willpower to questing as a bonus.

**Ori** (Lore) — Threat 8
Provides Lore sphere access for card draw and healing. Draws a card every time you play a Dwarf ally. Contributes willpower to questing. Keeps both decks' hands full throughout the game.

### Key cards for Deck B

| Card | Sphere | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| A Test of Will | Spirit | Cancel any encounter card when revealed — always hold one in hand |
| The Galadhrim's Greeting | Spirit | Reduce each player's threat by 3 — emergency valve when climbing |
| Unexpected Courage | Spirit | Second copy goes on Theodred — quest and pass resource same turn |
| Hasty Stroke | Spirit | Cancel a shadow card effect on a defended attack |
| Dunedain Warning | Leadership | +1 Defense attachment — put on Aragorn or Gimli |
| Gleowine | Lore | Ally: draw 1 card per round — free card draw engine |
| Sneak Attack + Gandalf | Leadership | Run Gandalf in both decks for double threat reduction access |

### How to play Deck B

**Every round:**
- Commit Eowyn + Theodred + as many allies as possible to questing
- Always pass Theodred's resource to Aragorn — never keep it in Deck B
- Hold A Test of Will in hand — cancel the first nasty Treachery revealed each round
- Use Ori to play Dwarf allies and trigger card draw

**Threat management:**
- Watch combined threat constantly — you start at 57
- Use Galadhrim's Greeting when either player hits 42+
- Sneak Attack Gandalf for -5 threat when either player hits 45+
- Gandalf for card draw or 4 damage only when threat is safely under 40
