## Deck A — The Three Hunters

### Heroes

**Aragorn** (Leadership) — Threat 12
After he commits to questing he readies himself — can then defend OR attack the same round. Your most versatile hero. Gets Steward of Gondor attached turn 1 — then generates 3 Leadership resources every round funding everything.

**Legolas** (Tactics) — Threat 9
Every kill places 2 progress tokens on the current quest. Combat = questing. The more enemies Legolas kills the faster you win quest stages without needing willpower. Equip weapons early.

**Gimli** (Tactics) — Threat 11
Gets +1 Attack for each damage token on him. Intentionally let him take hits — a Gimli with 3 damage is attacking for 5+. Pair with Dwarven Axe for devastating results. Best defender in Deck A.

### Deck A key cards

| Card | Sphere | Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Steward of Gondor | Leadership | ⭐ Turn 1 | On Aragorn — 3 resources/round, funds everything |
| Unexpected Courage | Spirit | Early | On Aragorn — quest + defend + attack same round |
| Dwarven Axe | Tactics | Early | On Gimli — boosts his attack further |
| Feint | Tactics | Always hold | Cancels an enemy attack entirely for the round |
| Quick Strike | Tactics | Situational | Attack before an enemy swings — kill it first |
| Sneak Attack + Gandalf | Leadership | Threat >40 | 1 resource for Gandalf's -5 threat option |
| Rivendell Blade | Tactics | Mid | On Legolas — lowers enemy defense, easier kills |

### How to play Deck A

**Turn 1:** Play Steward of Gondor on Aragorn immediately — nothing else matters more this turn. Commit Aragorn to quest, ready him with his ability, then use him to defend.

**Early game:** Let Gimli take a hit or two — every damage is +1 Attack. Use Feint on the biggest enemy attack each round. Get Legolas a weapon as soon as possible.

**Mid game:** Once Legolas has a weapon, kill chains drop 2 progress tokens per kill. You start winning quest stages through combat alone. Sneak Attack Gandalf for -5 threat whenever you hit 40+.

**Late game:** Gimli with 3+ damage + Dwarven Axe = one-shots most enemies. Aragorn with Unexpected Courage quests, readies, defends, attacks — does everything in one round.

## Deck B — The Support

### Heroes

**Éowyn** (Spirit) — Threat 9
Primary quester. Base 4 Willpower — highest in your entire pool. The Three Hunters barely quest so she carries it entirely. Her ability: discard any card from hand to boost Willpower by 1 — use this to push through tough quest stages or clear a big location in one round.

**Galadriel** (Neutral) — Threat 9
Cannot quest, attack, or defend — but every round she:
- Peeks at the top encounter card — move it to the bottom if it is nasty (enemy, treachery, shadow)
- Reduces your threat by 1 automatically — no card, no resource, no action needed
- With Nenya attached — gives +1 Willpower to every ally that enters play that round

Over 10 rounds that is -10 threat just from existing. She is your pressure valve that lets Aragorn's aggressive deck fight freely.

**Glorfindel** (Spirit) — Threat 5 ⭐
Only 5 threat — lowest hero in your entire pool. 3 Willpower + 3 Attack + 1 Defense. With Light of Valinor attached he does not exhaust to quest — he contributes willpower AND stays ready for combat. Extremely efficient hero.

### Deck B key cards

| Card | Sphere | Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light of Valinor | Spirit | ⭐ Turn 1 | On Glorfindel — quests without exhausting, stays ready for combat |
| Nenya | Neutral | Early | Galadriel's ring — +1 Willpower to allies entering play |
| A Test of Will | Spirit | Always hold 1 | Cancel any treachery card when revealed — priority target |
| Elrond's Counsel | Spirit | Threat >40 | -3 threat + 1 Willpower to a hero this round |
| Hasty Stroke | Spirit | Hold 1 | Cancel a shadow card effect — Deck A fights every round |
| Unexpected Courage | Spirit | Mid | Second copy — on Glorfindel so he can quest + attack |

### How to play Deck B

**Every round:**
- Commit Éowyn + Glorfindel (if Light of Valinor is on him, he stays ready too) + allies to questing
- Galadriel peeks the encounter deck — move nasty cards to the bottom, let safe ones through
- Hold A Test of Will in hand at all times — cancel the first Treachery revealed each round
- Galadriel's -1 threat fires automatically, no action needed

**Threat management:**
- Galadriel: -1 threat every round passively
- Elrond's Counsel: -3 threat when needed
- Sneak Attack Gandalf in Deck A: -5 threat in emergencies
- You should never threaten out on the first three core scenarios with this setup

## The Three Scenarios — This Weekend

### Scenario 1 — Passage Through Mirkwood
**Difficulty with this fellowship: Easy**
Great intro. Small enemy count, straightforward quest stages. Use this run to practice the Legolas kill → progress token combo and get comfortable with Galadriel's scry. You will win comfortably.

**Watch for:** Ungoliant's Spawn — high attack enemy. Feint it, then Gimli + Legolas kill it together.

### Scenario 2 — Journey Along the Anduin
**Difficulty with this fellowship: Medium**
The Hill Troll in the staging area at game start is the only real threat. Do NOT engage it until you are set up.

**Hill Troll strategy:**
1. Rounds 1-2: Ignore it, set up Steward of Gondor on Aragorn, get Legolas a weapon
2. Round 3: Engage it with Aragorn, use Feint to cancel its attack
3. Round 4: Gimli + Legolas + Aragorn (readied) all attack — it dies, Legolas drops 2 progress tokens from the kill

Once the Troll is dead the scenario is straightforward.

### Scenario 3 — Escape from Dol Guldur
**Difficulty with this fellowship: Hard**
One of your Deck A heroes gets captured at the start — you play with only 2 of the Three Hunters. Pray it is not Aragorn.

**Tips:**
- Play very defensively — do not engage extra enemies
- Keep threat as low as possible — Galadriel's scry is even more valuable here
- If Aragorn is captured it is very tough — consider restarting
- If Gimli is captured — Aragorn + Legolas can still function, Legolas carries combat
- Focus on completing quest stages fast rather than killing everything
