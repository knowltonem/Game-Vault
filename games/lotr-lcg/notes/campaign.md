# Campaign Mode

← [[games/lotr-lcg/index|Back to LOTR LCG]]

Campaign mode is new to the **Revised Core Set** (not present in the original). It lets you chain scenarios together as a linked narrative, carrying consequences forward.

---

## How it works

Before your first campaign scenario, set up a **campaign log** (a piece of paper or a note works fine). Track:
- Which boons and burdens each hero is carrying
- Completed scenarios
- Any story choices made

---

## The three card types

**Campaign cards** — set up special rules that apply across the whole campaign. Usually one or two are active at a time, modifying how certain things work.

**Boon cards** — permanent upgrades you earn for victories or positive story outcomes. Attached to specific heroes and carry into every future scenario. Examples: improved stats, special abilities, extra resources.

**Burden cards** — ongoing penalties for failures or story choices. Also attached to heroes. Can stack up and make later scenarios harder. Examples: ongoing threat raises, card restrictions, forced damage.

---

## Campaign flow (Revised Core Set)

The Revised Core Set contains a 3-scenario campaign:

1. **Passage Through Mirkwood** *(introductory)*
2. **Journey Along the Anduin** *(moderate)*
3. **Escape from Dol Guldur** *(hard)*

The campaign then continues into expansion products that include dedicated campaign cards:

| Product | Scenarios added |
|---|---|
| [[games/lotr-lcg/buying/products#dark-of-mirkwood\|The Dark of Mirkwood]] | 2 scenarios |
| Angmar Awakened Campaign Expansion | Full cycle campaign |
| Dream-chaser Campaign Expansion | Full cycle campaign |
| Ered Mithrin Campaign Expansion | Full cycle campaign |
| Fellowship of the Ring Saga | 6 scenarios |
| The Two Towers Saga | 6 scenarios |
| The Return of the King Saga | 6 scenarios |

---

## Tips for campaign play

- Don't rush into the Saga expansions early — the scenarios are significantly harder and the campaign payoff is best when your deck is strong
- Heroes can die in campaign mode (depending on scenario rules) — manage threat carefully
- Some burden cards persist across multiple expansions; read them carefully before accepting story choices
- You can replay individual scenarios outside of campaign mode to practice without risking your campaign state
