# Game Setup — Step by Step

← [[games/lotr-lcg/notes/rules|Rules & Turn Phases]] | [[games/lotr-lcg/index|Back to LOTR LCG]]

> Complete setup guide for the Revised Core Set. Covers solo, two-player, and two-handed solo. Follow these steps in order every time you sit down to play.

---

## The Card Types and Where They Go

Before setup makes sense, you need to know what each card type is and which area of the table it belongs to.

### Player cards (your cards)

| Card type | What it looks like | Where it goes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero cards** | Larger portrait, stat bar across the bottom | Hero area in front of you, face up |
| **Ally cards** | Blue/red/green/purple sphere icon top left | Your player deck (shuffled) |
| **Attachment cards** | Same sphere icons, attach to heroes or allies | Your player deck |
| **Event cards** | Same sphere icons, one-time effects | Your player deck |

### Encounter cards (the game's cards — your enemy)

| Card type | What it looks like | Where it goes |
|---|---|---|
| **Enemy cards** | Red combat stats across the bottom | Encounter deck or staging area |
| **Location cards** | Blue quest progress number | Encounter deck or staging area |
| **Treachery cards** | No stats — just a nasty effect | Encounter deck |
| **Shadow cards** | Dark art, shadow effect at bottom | Encounter deck (used face down) |

### Quest cards

| Card type | What it looks like | Where it goes |
|---|---|---|
| **Quest cards** | Large card, Part A on one side / Part B reverse | Quest deck, face down stack |

## Step 1 — Choose Your Scenario

Pick which scenario you're playing. For your first game use **Passage Through Mirkwood** — it's the easiest and designed as the introduction.

Each scenario uses a specific set of encounter cards. The Learn to Play booklet lists exactly which encounter sets belong to each scenario. Find those cards and set the rest aside — do not mix encounter sets from different scenarios.

**Passage Through Mirkwood encounter sets:**
- Passage Through Mirkwood (the scenario-specific cards)
- Spiders of Mirkwood
- Wilderland

---

## Step 2 — Build the Quest Deck

Find the quest cards for your chosen scenario. They are numbered on the back — 1A, 1B, 2A, 2B, 3A, 3B etc.

Stack them in order with **1A on top**, face down. Do not shuffle. This is your quest deck — you'll flip through it in order as you complete each stage.

Place the quest deck face down in the centre of the table in the **quest area**.

---

## Step 3 — Build the Encounter Deck

Take all the encounter cards for your chosen scenario (enemies, locations, treacheries). Shuffle them all together into one face-down deck. This is the **encounter deck**.

Place it face down in the quest area next to the quest deck.

> Important: Keep any cards listed as "setup" cards separate — some scenarios tell you to start with specific enemies or locations already in play before shuffling the rest. Read the quest card 1A carefully for setup instructions.

---

## Step 4 — Reveal the First Quest Card

Flip the top quest card (1A) face up. Read it carefully. It will tell you:

- Any special setup instructions for the scenario
- What the quest requires to complete (usually a progress number)
- Any special rules in effect for this stage

Follow any setup instructions on 1A before continuing.

---

## Step 5 — Set Up the Staging Area

The **staging area** is the shared space in the centre of the table between the quest deck and the players. Enemies and locations that have been revealed but not yet engaged go here.

Some scenarios instruct you to place specific cards in the staging area during setup. Check quest card 1A. Passage Through Mirkwood for example starts with one location already revealed in the staging area.

---

## Step 6 — Choose Your Heroes

Each player selects exactly **3 heroes** and places them face up in their **hero area** (the space in front of you on your side of the table).

For the recommended Two-Handed Solo fellowship:
- **Deck A heroes:** Aragorn, Legolas, Gimli — place face up on the left side
- **Deck B heroes:** Éowyn, Théodred, Ori — place face up on the right side

Heroes start the game **ready** (upright, not exhausted).

---

## Step 7 — Set Starting Threat

Each player sets their **threat dial** to the combined threat cost of their 3 heroes.

| Fellowship | Heroes | Starting threat |
|---|---|---|
| Deck A | Aragorn (12) + Legolas (9) + Gimli (11) | **32** |
| Deck B | Éowyn (9) + Théodred (8) + Ori (8) | **25** |

Set Deck A's dial to 32 and Deck B's dial to 25.

> If either player's threat ever reaches 50, they are eliminated. Track this carefully every round.

---

## Step 8 — Build and Draw Your Opening Hand

Each player builds their **player deck** from their chosen cards (allies, attachments, events). Shuffle it thoroughly.

**Do not include hero cards in your player deck** — heroes are set aside in your hero area, never in the deck.

Each player draws **6 cards** as their opening hand.

You may mulligan once: if you don't like your hand, shuffle all 6 cards back into your deck and draw 6 new cards. You must keep the second hand.

**What to look for in your opening hand:**
- Deck A: Steward of Gondor is the dream keep — if you have it, keep the hand
- Deck B: A Test of Will is always worth keeping — hold it every game
- Both: At least 2–3 playable cards (cost ≤ your starting resources)

---

## Step 9 — Place Starting Resources

Each hero starts with **1 resource token** on their hero card in the **resource pool**.

Place 1 token on each hero:
- Aragorn: 1 Leadership resource
- Legolas: 1 Tactics resource
- Gimli: 1 Tactics resource
- Éowyn: 1 Spirit resource
- Théodred: 1 Leadership resource
- Ori: 1 Lore resource

Resources match the sphere icon on the hero card. You can only spend a hero's resources on cards matching their sphere (unless a card says otherwise).

---

## Step 10 — Determine First Player

Choose who goes first. Place the **First Player token** in front of that player. In two-handed solo, start with Deck A.

The First Player token passes clockwise each round. In two-handed solo, alternate between Deck A and Deck B each round.

## Common Setup Mistakes

**Mistake: Putting hero cards in the player deck**
Heroes are never in the player deck. They live in your hero area permanently until eliminated.

**Mistake: Mixing up encounter sets**
Each scenario uses only specific encounter card sets. Check the Learn to Play for the exact list. Using the wrong encounter cards makes scenarios much harder or easier than intended.

**Mistake: Starting resources on heroes**
Each hero starts with exactly 1 resource. Not 0, not 2 — always 1 each.

**Mistake: Shuffling the quest deck**
Quest cards go in numbered order, never shuffled. 1A is always on top.

**Mistake: Drawing 7 cards instead of 6**
Opening hand is 6 cards. The rules say 6. It's easy to accidentally draw 7.

**Mistake: Forgetting setup cards**
Always read quest card 1A completely before play. Many scenarios place enemies or locations in the staging area before the first round starts. Missing this makes the game easier than intended.

**Mistake: Not reading both sides of quest card 1A**
The A side sets up the scenario. The B side (face down) is the back of stage 1 — you flip to it when you advance. Don't read the B side until you advance.

## Two-Handed Solo Specific Notes

When playing two-handed solo (controlling both decks yourself):

- Manage each deck completely independently — separate hero areas, separate player decks, separate threat dials, separate hands
- Both decks draw 6 cards and set starting threat separately
- The First Player token starts with Deck A and alternates each round
- Deck A takes all its actions for a phase before Deck B takes its actions in the same phase
- Both decks contribute willpower to the same quest total
- Both decks face enemies from the same encounter deck and staging area
- Either deck can engage any enemy in the staging area on their turn

## Quick Setup Checklist

- [ ] Choose scenario and find correct encounter card sets
- [ ] Build quest deck in numbered order (1A on top, face down)
- [ ] Shuffle encounter deck, place face down
- [ ] Flip quest card 1A face up, read all setup instructions
- [ ] Place any setup cards in staging area per 1A instructions
- [ ] Set heroes face up in hero area (not in player deck)
- [ ] Set threat dials (Deck A: 32, Deck B: 25)
- [ ] Shuffle player decks
- [ ] Draw 6 cards each (mulligan once if needed)
- [ ] Place 1 resource token on each hero
- [ ] Place First Player token with Deck A
- [ ] Begin Round 1 — Resource Phase
