## Beginner fundamentals

**Don't neglect questing.** Winning requires making progress on the quest. Many new players over-invest in combat and let staging area threat spiral out of control.

**Threat management is everything.** Once your threat climbs, enemies auto-engage and pile up. Spirit sphere cards like *A Test of Will* and *The Galadhrim's Greeting* exist precisely to keep this under control.

**Commit characters carefully.** A character committed to questing is exhausted — it can't defend that round. Leaving defenders ready is often more important than maximum questing power.

**Chump blocking is valid.** A cheap ally can defend an attack and absorb damage, saving your heroes. Don't feel bad about it.

**Shadow cards matter.** Some shadow effects are devastating. Cards that cancel or discard shadow cards (*Secret Vigil*, *Hasty Stroke*) are very powerful.

## Deck building

**Start with 2 spheres.** Spreading across 3+ spheres makes resource generation inconsistent, especially early.

**Recommended beginner combo — Spirit + Lore:**
- Spirit: Éowyn (high willpower), threat reduction, event cancellation
- Lore: card draw, healing, deck consistency
- Result: smooth questing, threat under control, cards always flowing

**Include about:**
- 15–20 allies
- 10–15 attachments
- 10–15 events
- Aim for 50 cards total (minimum)

**3 copies of key cards.** The game allows up to 3 copies of each non-unique card. If a card is central to your strategy, run 3.

**RingsDB** (ringsdb.com) — search the full card pool, filter by sphere, and browse community decks. Essential for deck building before you know all the cards.

## Solo vs. multiplayer

**Solo:** You control 2 separate decks (2-handed solo) or 1 deck. Threat management is harder solo because you reveal fewer encounter cards per round but have less resources. Spirit + Lore is king here.

**Multiplayer:** More cards revealed per round but more resources and actions. Players can specialize — one player goes full Tactics (combat), another goes Spirit/Lore (questing/support). Communication is key.

## Scenario difficulty

The three core set scenarios scale in difficulty:

| Scenario | Difficulty | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Passage Through Mirkwood | ⭐ Easy | Good intro, forgiving |
| Journey Along the Anduin | ⭐⭐⭐ Medium-Hard | Hill Troll opening can end runs fast |
| Escape from Dol Guldur | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very Hard | One hero is captured at start — brutal |

> [!warning] Escape from Dol Guldur is genuinely hard even for experienced players. Don't feel bad losing it many times.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to raise threat by 1 at the end of Refresh phase
- Not paying attention to the active location's quest points before adding progress to the quest card
- Engaging enemies before you have a ready defender
- Playing out your whole hand in Planning before committing to the quest — leave some cards for action windows
