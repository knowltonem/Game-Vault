# Boons & Burdens

← [[games/lotr-lcg/notes/campaign|Campaign Mode]] | [[games/lotr-lcg/index|Back to LOTR LCG]]

> Boons and Burdens are persistent campaign cards that heroes carry between scenarios. Boons are positive upgrades earned through success. Burdens are penalties earned through failure or difficult choices. Together they make your campaign feel like a living story — heroes grow stronger, but the journey leaves its mark.

---

## The Core Concept

In standalone play (one scenario at a time), you ignore all campaign cards entirely.

In **campaign mode**, every scenario is connected. What happens in scenario 1 affects scenario 2. Heroes carry Boons and Burdens between sessions like permanent attachments. By the end of a campaign your heroes are measurably different from how they started.

---

## Boon Cards

Boons are **positive permanent attachments** earned during a campaign. They represent skills learned, items found, relationships formed, and titles earned.

### How you earn them
Campaign quest cards contain instructions like:
> "If the players completed the bonus objective, add [Boon Name] to the victory display."

Or at the end of a scenario:
> "The first player may attach [Boon Name] to a hero."

Some boons are awarded automatically for completing a scenario. Others require completing optional side objectives.

### How they work in play
- After earning a boon, attach it to a specific hero as a permanent attachment
- When you set up the **next scenario**, that hero starts with the boon already attached — no cost, no card draw required, just in play from round 1
- Boons function like normal attachments (stat boosts, new abilities, triggered effects) but they can never be discarded by card effects — they are permanent

### Boon types in the Revised Core Set

| Type | What it represents | Effect style |
|---|---|---|
| Title | A name or rank earned | Usually a stat boost or passive ability |
| Item | A weapon, armour, or tool found | Equipment bonuses, new actions |
| Skill | Something learned or mastered | Triggered abilities, once-per-round effects |

### Key Boons to know

**Sword that was Broken** — Title boon for Aragorn. Gives +1 Willpower and lets him quest without exhausting once per round. Transforms him from a good hero into an exceptional one — quests for free and still readies to fight.

**Celebrimbor's Secret** — Item boon. Attachment that lets a hero heal damage each round passively. Solves the no-healer problem in the core campaign.

**Tireless Ranger** — Skill boon. Lets a hero ready during the refresh phase instead of needing Unexpected Courage.

## Burden Cards

Burdens are **negative permanent attachments** that accumulate from failures, hard choices, or encounter card effects during campaign play.

### How you earn them
- Failing to complete a scenario objective
- Making certain choices in campaign branch points
- Specific encounter cards that add burdens as part of their effect
- Having a hero knocked out during certain scenarios

### How they work in play
- Attach to a hero the same way boons do — permanently
- Burdens range from minor inconveniences (+1 threat per round) to serious handicaps (hero cannot gain resources from certain spheres)
- Some trigger during setup, some affect gameplay each round, some activate under specific conditions
- They **cannot be removed** during normal play — you carry them for the rest of the campaign

### Key Burdens to know

**Overcome by Grief** — Hero loses 1 hit point from their maximum health permanently.

**Stinging Flies** — Hero exhausts at the start of the planning phase, losing their first action each round.

**Lost in the Dark** — Hero cannot contribute willpower to questing until a condition is met.

## Critical Rules

### Boons are lost if a hero dies
If a hero is eliminated during a scenario (reaches 0 hit points), **all boons attached to that hero are permanently removed from the campaign**. This makes hero death genuinely costly — you lose not just the hero for that scenario but everything they've built up. Protect your boon-holders.

### One boon/burden per award moment
Each campaign reward moment gives you one boon (or burden). You cannot stack multiple boons from the same event.

### You choose the attachment target
Unless specifically stated, you choose which hero receives a boon. Plan ahead — some boons are clearly better on specific heroes (Sword that was Broken only makes sense on Aragorn).

### Burdens are distributed by the first player
When a burden must be assigned, the first player chooses which hero receives it. Spread burdens across heroes rather than stacking them on one.

### Campaign vs standalone
Boons and Burdens cards are **only used in campaign mode**. In standalone scenarios, remove all campaign cards from the box before play — they are not used.

## The Revised Core Set Campaign

The Revised Core Set contains a 3-scenario mini-campaign:

| Order | Scenario | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Passage Through Mirkwood | Easiest — best boon opportunities, good starting point |
| 2 | Journey Along the Anduin | Medium — boons from scenario 1 help here |
| 3 | Escape from Dol Guldur | Hardest — one hero is captured at setup; boons from earlier scenarios are crucial |

Boons earned in Passage Through Mirkwood carry into Journey Along the Anduin. Boons earned across both carry into Escape from Dol Guldur. A well-run campaign means your heroes are noticeably stronger by the final scenario.

## Tips for Managing Boons & Burdens

**Prioritise Aragorn for title boons.** He benefits most from stat upgrades since he's your most flexible hero. Sword that was Broken in particular makes him significantly better.

**Protect your boon-holders in combat.** Never use a hero loaded with boons as a reckless tank. Keep them alive at all costs.

**Spread burdens across the party.** If Aragorn already has a burden, assign the next one to Gimli or Legolas — don't pile them on one hero.

**Take notes between sessions.** Use [[games/lotr-lcg/collection/my-collection|My Collection]] or a campaign log to track which boons and burdens each hero carries. It's easy to forget between play sessions.

**Play Passage Through Mirkwood on Normal before campaign mode.** Learn the scenario mechanics in standalone first, then run it as campaign once you're comfortable.
