# Legacy (Chapter 1) Content — What to Grab for Early Play

← [[games/arkham-lcg/index|Back to Arkham LCG]] · See also [[games/arkham-lcg/buying/guide|Buying guide]]

> Context: I'm starting from the **Core Set (2026)** = "Chapter Two." Everything before it is the **legacy / Chapter 1** pool — still playable and backwards-compatible, but with important catches below. Sourced from DerBK's *Ancient Evils* guides (the community's most respected resource, updated for Chapter 2).

---

## The key distinction: player cards vs. scenarios

"Backwards compatible" splits into two very different answers:

| | Verdict for a new Chapter 2 player |
|---|---|
| **Old player cards / investigators** | **Skip for now.** Chapter 2 rebuilds the card pool from scratch and rebalances old problem cards. Mixing in Chapter 1 cards undercuts that and buys redundancy. You don't *need* any of it. |
| **Old campaigns / scenarios** | **Worth grabbing.** No full 8-part campaign shipped for 2026 — only two 3-part mini-campaigns exist in Chapter 2 so far. The legacy campaigns fill that gap. |

---

## Must-have investigators? (mostly a trap early)

There's no meta to chase — there are **no bad investigators**, only narrower ones. Don't buy old investigators for power.

**The one exception worth considering:** the old **Investigator Starter Decks** (self-contained, so they bolt onto the Chapter 2 pool cleanly):

| Investigator | Class | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Stella Clark** | Survivor | Possibly the best all-rounder in the game; resilient, recovers actions from failed tests. Safest pick. |
| **Winifred Habbamock** | Rogue | High-risk/high-reward; fills the otherwise-thin early Rogue pool. |
| **Nathaniel Cho** | Guardian | The purest fighter — does little else, but does it very well. |
| Harvey Walters | Seeker | "Big hand" card-draw engine; frail, needs protection. |
| Jacqueline Fine | Mystic | Chaos-bag manipulation; rounds out the Mystic toolbox. |

> ⚠️ Caveat: Chapter 2 is expected to revisit these same themes soon, which may make them redundant. The **intended** path is the new Chapter 2 investigator decks (Tommy, Carolyn, André, Marie, Miguel).

## Can't-miss horror stories (the real answer)

Priority order for a new Chapter 2 player:

1. **The Path to Carcosa** ⭐ — *Best story.* Based on *The King in Yellow*; insanity, possession, fourth-wall tricks. Challenges are even across skills, so it's forgiving when your card pool is shallow. **Best first legacy campaign.**
2. **The Dunwich Legacy** — *Gentlest step up.* Grounded mechanically but shows the game's depth. Necronomicon, Yog-Sothoth. Great alternative first pick.
3. **The Edge of the Earth** — *At the Mountains of Madness.* Antarctic expedition, 10 scenarios (longest campaign), split-party mechanic, bonded cards. **Easiest campaign to convert to Chapter 2** — only 4 core encounter sets used. See the [[games/arkham-lcg/notes/edge-of-the-earth-guide|full substitution guide]]. ⚠️ Campaign Expansion is **out of print** — buy if you find it at reasonable price.
4. **The Innsmouth Conspiracy** — *Shadow over Innsmouth.* Fish-people, combat-heavy, huge maps (big table). Fine first buy; consider Easy.
5. **The Feast of Hemlock Vale** — *Modern showcase.* Folk horror (*Colour Out of Space*, *Midsommar*, *Annihilation*). Nonlinear, fair but challenging. Later pickup.
6. **The Forgotten Age** — *Best structure, DerBK's favorite,* but **infamously hard — not a first buy.** Time travel, parallel worlds, endless snakes. Get the **Return to the Forgotten Age** box too — it smooths the difficulty spikes and is the definitive version.

**Avoid as a first legacy pickup:**
- **Circle Undone** & **Scarlet Keys** — too hard / too much bookkeeping for beginners.
- **Dream-Eaters** — two mini-campaigns, not one full one; undersells the game's progression.

**Standalone one-shots (hidden gems):** top tier is **Murder at the Excelsior Hotel** (wake up with a bloody knife beside a corpse), **The Blob That Ate Everything**, and **Carnivale of Horrors**.

## ⚠️ The logistics catch for Chapter 2 owners

Old campaigns need the **encounter sets from the Old Core** — which the 2026 Core does **not** include. Three ways around it:

1. **Print-and-play** — FFG released the old core encounter sets free on their site. Cheapest fix.
2. **DerBK's "better replacements"** — per-scenario swaps to play legacy campaigns with Chapter 2 encounter sets. Most campaigns port easily; **Circle Undone and Dream-Eaters are the two that don't.** (Use this over FFG's official substitution table, which he found lacking.)
3. **Buy a cheap Old Core** (~$15–60 used) just for the 50 encounter cards — usually only worth it if you find a steal.

→ Carcosa, **Edge of the Earth**, Dunwich, Forgotten Age, Innsmouth, and Hemlock Vale all port cleanly. Avoid Circle Undone / Dream-Eaters as *first* legacy pickups specifically because they're hardest to convert.

## My plan

1. Play the Core's *Brethren of Ash* campaign first.
2. First legacy campaign: **Carcosa** (story) or **Dunwich** (gentlest ramp).
3. Port it using DerBK's replacement guide (or FFG print-and-play encounter sets).
4. Hold off on old investigators/player cards until Chapter 2 fills out.
