## Scenario-by-scenario substitutions

### Ice and Death (Parts 1, 2, and 3)

| Original (Old Core) | FFG Suggests | DerBK Suggests |
|---|---|---|
| Ancient Evils | Cosmic Evils | **Cosmic Evils** |

Straight swap.

### Fatal Mirage

| Original | FFG Suggests | DerBK Suggests |
|---|---|---|
| Chilling Cold | Bad Weather | **Bad Weather** |

Straight swap.

### To the Forbidden Peaks

No core encounter sets needed. No substitutions required.

### City of the Elder Things

| Original | FFG Suggests | DerBK Suggests |
|---|---|---|
| Locked Doors | Arcane Lock | **Eldritch Lore** |
| Chilling Cold | Bad Weather | **Reeking Decay** |

> **Why:** Arcane Lock works, but Eldritch Lore is a better fit — it preserves the function of hindering clue-gatherers. Reeking Decay replaces Bad Weather because the campaign shifts from "ice is killing us" to "miasma is killing us" in the back half, and Reeking Decay fits the miasma flavor perfectly.

### Heart of Madness, Part 1

| Original | FFG Suggests | DerBK Suggests |
|---|---|---|
| Ancient Evils | Cosmic Evils | **Cosmic Evils** |
| Locked Doors | Arcane Lock | **Arcane Lock** |

### Heart of Madness, Part 2

| Original | FFG Suggests | DerBK Suggests |
|---|---|---|
| Ancient Evils | Cosmic Evils | **Cosmic Evils** |
| Chilling Cold | Bad Weather | **Reeking Decay** |
| Striking Fear (without Rotting Remains) | Torment (full set?) | **Torment** (drop the Torment card itself) |

> **Why:** Reeking Decay again fits the miasma theme. For Striking Fear, the original uses only Frozen in Fear and Dissonant Voices (leaving out Rotting Remains). Torment doesn't have a direct Rotting Remains analogue, so use the full set — or if you want to trim, remove the Torment card (Languor and Dissonance are the best Frozen in Fear replacements).

## What's special about Edge of the Earth

- **First campaign in the new format** (Campaign + Investigator Expansions) — meaning it was designed for the all-in-one-box structure
- **10 scenarios** — the longest campaign in the game (most are 8)
- **The "choose your own path" system** — branching routes through Antarctica that change which scenarios you play
- **Split party mechanic** — investigators can be in different locations and take separate actions
- **Bonded / weakness-heavy design** — the first campaign to heavily feature the bonded card mechanic
- **Thematic masterpiece** — genuine tension between scientific expedition and cosmic horror, with a strong *At the Mountains of Madness* vibe

From DerBK: *"Out of all the campaigns available for Chapter 1, Edge of the Earth is the one that uses the fewest Core encounter sets. Only four of them are used and they can be substituted fairly smoothly with the Chapter 2 offerings. This is a campaign that is very easy to adapt."*
