## What is an LCG?

A **Living Card Game** is a fixed-distribution card game — unlike a CCG, every copy of a product contains the exact same cards. No randomized boosters, no rarity chase. If you want a card, buy the product it's in and you're guaranteed to get it.

## What makes Arkham different

Arkham is the most **narrative and campaign-focused** of FFG's card games. It's built around **campaigns**: a linked series of scenarios forming a story. Investigators persist between scenarios — they earn **experience (XP)** to upgrade decks and accumulate **physical and mental trauma** that carries forward. The story branches on success, failure, and your choices. Closer to a legacy-lite RPG in card form than a one-off battler.

## The core loop

You play an **investigator** with a custom deck. Each scenario is driven by game-controlled decks: the **act/agenda decks** push the story, and the **encounter deck** throws enemies and treacheries at you. The central tension is a race — the **act deck** is your progress toward winning (clue-driven), while the **agenda deck** is the doom clock advancing against you.

## What's in your deck

| Card type | What it does |
|---|---|
| Investigator | Your character; defines stats, deckbuilding rules, special ability |
| Assets | Allies, items, weapons, spells that stay in play |
| Events | One-shot effects played from hand, then discarded |
| Skills | Committed to skill tests for bonuses, then discarded |
| Weaknesses | Mandatory bad cards shuffled into your deck |

## The four core stats

| Stat | Used for |
|---|---|
| **Willpower** | Resisting treacheries, casting spells |
| **Intellect** | Investigating locations to find clues |
| **Combat** | Fighting and damaging enemies |
| **Agility** | Evading enemies, dodging hazards |

## Skill tests & the chaos bag

Instead of dice, Arkham uses a **chaos bag** of tokens. On a skill test you draw a token at random and modify your stat by its value (often negative); commit skill cards to boost. Beat the difficulty and you succeed. The bag includes the **auto-fail (tentacle)**, the **Elder Sign**, and campaign-specific symbol tokens.
