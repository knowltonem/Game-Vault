## Win & loss conditions

**Win:** Advance through the **act deck** to its resolution by gathering and spending clues.

**Resolution triggers:** the **agenda deck** advances to its final stage (doom runs out); all investigators are **defeated**; or a scenario-specific failure condition is met.

> In Arkham, "losing" a scenario usually doesn't end the campaign — you read a different **resolution** and the story continues, often with consequences.

## Defeat: damage & horror

Each investigator has **Health** (reduced by **damage** → physical trauma) and **Sanity** (reduced by **horror** → mental trauma). Hitting 0 in either defeats you for the rest of the scenario and records trauma.

## The 4 phases of each round

**1 — Mythos:** place 1 doom on the agenda (advances if at threshold); each investigator draws and resolves 1 encounter card.
**2 — Investigation:** each investigator takes **3 actions** — investigate, move, fight, evade, play a card, activate an ability, draw, or gain a resource.
**3 — Enemy:** each ready engaged enemy attacks; unengaged enemies move/engage.
**4 — Upkeep:** ready exhausted cards, draw 1, gain 1 resource, discard to hand size (8).

## Key concepts

**Clues** advance the act deck. **Doom** accrues on the agenda and advances the threat. **Act vs Agenda** is the two-sided race. **Engaging/exhausting** — an engaged enemy attacks you each Enemy phase; exhausted cards ready in Upkeep. **Chaos bag** is your randomizer. **Resources** pay for cards (1 per Upkeep).

## Campaign mode

Played as **campaigns** — linked scenarios where you earn **XP** (spend between scenarios to upgrade cards), accumulate **trauma**, and follow a **branching story** tracked on the **Campaign Log**.
