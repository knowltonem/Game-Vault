## Chapter Two (2026) compatibility

**Yes, works fine.** Outpost 31 is fully self-contained — it includes all its own encounter cards (Act & Agenda, Locations, Story Assets, Treachery, Enemies, Blood Tests set). You only need player cards, tokens, and a chaos bag from a core set. Chapter Two provides all of those.

## Placement with Edge of the Earth

Best used as a **prologue** before the campaign begins. Play it as a side-story inserted into the campaign — XP carries forward, trauma carries forward.

### The hook — why you're in Antarctica

The investigators aren't explorers. They're a **rescue team**.

Miskatonic University's remote research station — Outpost 31 — has gone dark. Seventeen scientists and support staff. Last transmission was fragmented, panicked, and cut short by static. A ski-equipped Fairchild monoplane is dispatched from the coastal supply depot, its skis rattling across the ice shelf as it touches down near the station's frozen perimeter. The investigators step out into −40° wind chill and silence.

What they find at Outpost 31 is not the beginning of a separate story. It's the first chapter of the same one.

### Narrative connections to Edge of the Earth

| Outpost 31 element | Connects to EotE... | How |
|---|---|---|
| The "organism" — a shape-shifting biological horror | **City of the Elder Things** | The organism is an escaped Elder Thing experiment — a guardian organism left behind when the city was sealed. The blood tests, quarantine protocols, and biological horror you encounter here are the first hints of what dwells beneath the ice. |
| Dr. Helena Voss, Outpost 31's lead biologist | *(implied)* | Her research notes mention "unusual cellular structures" in ice core samples — the same ice cores that led the main EotE expedition to the ancient city. She was closer to the truth than anyone knew. |
| The station's seismic readings | **Ice and Death** | Outpost 31 recorded low-frequency vibrations from deep beneath the ice in the weeks before contact was lost. The main expedition dismisses these as glacial movement. They're not. |
| A survivor's journal — "the city... towers under the ice... they're still here" | **To the Forbidden Peaks / City of the Elder Things** | The ramblings of a now-missing station meteorologist point directly toward the Elder Things ruins. His last known heading matches the expedition's route. |
| Survivors who fled into the blizzard | **Fatal Mirage** | The bodies (or what's left of them) found in this scenario could be Outpost 31 personnel who tried to escape on foot. The organism doesn't need hosts to survive the cold. |
| Shared nightmares reported by infected victims | **The Search for Kadath** | The organism's victims experience identical dreams — a vast, cyclopean city of non-Euclidean geometry beneath a green-lit sky. A faint psychic echo of the Elder Things' collective memory, bleeding through the ice. |
| Blood samples showing non-terrestrial DNA | **Heart of Madness, Part 2** | The samples don't match anything in known biology. They match the tissue samples found later in the Elder Things' laboratory. The organism wasn't created here. It was **rediscovered**. |
| The station's radio operator — last words: "it's not the cold we should be afraid of" | *(campaign tone)* | Sets the emotional register for the entire campaign. The enemy isn't the environment. The enemy is what lives in it. |

### Thematic foreshadowing

Outpost 31 primes the player for everything Edge of the Earth does best:

- **Paranoia and trust** — the organism's mimicry prefigures the campaign's moments where investigators must rely on each other in impossible circumstances
- **Isolation** — the frozen, corpse-littered corridors of Outpost 31 establish Antarctica as beautiful, hostile, and utterly indifferent to human survival
- **Ancient horror** — the sense that *something* was here long before humans, was sealed away, and is now stirring again
- **Body horror** — the biological intimacy of the organism's infection mirrors the Elder Things' genetic meddling and the campaign's bonded/weakness mechanics
- **Knowledge costs** — every clue gained at Outpost 31 comes at a price, setting the pattern for the campaign's moral choices

### Recommended campaign order

1. **Outpost 31** — prologue, inserted as a side-story before the campaign begins
2. **Ice and Death, Parts 1–3** — arrive at Miskatonic Base, Outpost 31's sister station
3. **Fatal Mirage** or **To the Forbidden Peaks** — following the trail the Outpost 31 dead left behind
4. **City of the Elder Things** — the origin of the organism is revealed
5. **Heart of Madness, Parts 1–2** — the truth at the end of the ice

### Standalone mode

Can also be played standalone (no campaign) whenever you want a short Antarctic horror session. No Edge of the Earth knowledge required.

## 1920s period accuracy

Edge of the Earth is set in the **1920s** (ship from Boston through the Panama Canal, ski-equipped aeroplanes, dog sleds, radio). Outpost 31's card art is modern — it was designed as a standalone scenario, not as an Edge of the Earth prequel. When playing as a prologue, mentally re-flavor these elements for consistency:

| Card / Element | Issue | 1920s re-flavor |
|---|---|---|
| **Helicopter-Pad** (location) | Helicopters didn't exist in 1920s Antarctica | **Landing Strip** or **Ice Runway** — the ski-plane is parked here |
| **Vehicle-Storage** (location) | Implies modern vehicles | Houses the ski-plane, fuel drums, and dog sleds |
| **Station Protocol** (treachery) | "Protocols" is modern jargon | **Station Procedures** or **Standing Orders** |
| **Repair Protocols** (treachery) | Same issue | **Repair Orders** or **Maintenance Schedule** |

**Things that are period-accurate:**
- **Flamethrower** (story asset) — existed since WWI (1915), used by early Antarctic expeditions for melting ice
- **Radio-room** (location) — radio communication was standard in 1920s exploration
- **Blood tests, medical quarantine, autopsies** — all existed in 1920s medicine
- Character names (MacReady, Childs, etc.) — period-neutral
- Dog kennels — dog sleds were the primary Antarctic transport
